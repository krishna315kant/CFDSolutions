Solver Development

During my Ph.D., I developed a multiphase solver to improve the accuracy of interfacial flow simulations. One of the major challenges in numerical simulations of multiphase flows is the presence of spurious currents, which can lead to inaccuracies in phase interface tracking. To address this issue, I implemented smoothing techniques and a close point search method, which effectively reduced spurious currents by 2â€“3 orders of magnitude.

The solver was extensively validated against benchmark cases, and the improvements significantly enhanced the reliability of simulations involving droplet interactions, interface stability, and phase transitions.

Atomization

My Ph.D. research also focused on atomization physics, particularly the breakup of droplets in high-shear environments. I developed a generalized correlation for the critical Weber number that predicts droplet breakup across a wide range of Ohnesorge numbers and density ratios.

Additionally, I improved the internal flow theory to predict droplet deformation rates, maximum deformation, and breakup mechanisms. This work plays a crucial role in optimizing spray processes in combustion and industrial applications where precise droplet behavior prediction is required.

Flash Boiling

My postdoctoral research focuses on flash boiling, a phenomenon occurring when a superheated liquid undergoes rapid phase transition due to a pressure drop. I am currently working with CONVERGE CFD, utilizing the Real Fluid Model to capture the complex behavior of metastable states in flash boiling.

The goal of my work is to develop a more accurate model for metastable phase transitions, which is crucial for applications in fuel injection systems, cryogenic storage, and space propulsion technologies.

Quantum Computing

I am currently exploring the potential of quantum computing in fluid dynamics modeling. To build a strong foundation in this field, I have enrolled in a quantum computing foundation course offered by QpiAI.

My aim is to understand how quantum algorithms can enhance the efficiency and accuracy of complex multiphase flow simulations, particularly in scenarios where classical methods face computational bottlenecks.
